What is Climate Change?

Climate change is a change in the weather found in a specific place. This could be a change in how much rain falls in a certain region. Or it could be a change in a place's usual temperature of a season.

Climate change is a long-term change in the average weather patterns that have come to define Earth’s local, regional and global climates.

The term Climate sometimes gets mixed with the term Weather, Weather is the changes we see and feel outside from day to day. Climate in the otherhand is the longterm changes a place experiences.

What Is Causing Earth's Climate to Change?

Geological records show that there have been a number of large variations in Earth’s climate. These have been caused by many natural factors, this change has typically occurred very slowly over thousands or millions of years. However, research shows that the current climate is changing more rapidly than shown in geological records.

10 Things You Can Do To Help Lessen the Impact of Climate Change

1. Stop using Disposables

Most Disposables are made up of Plastic, and as we know, these plastics last for a very long time and when thrown out, contributes to the greenhouse emmisions that worsens global warming.

2. Avoid wasting food

Food waste is a big problem since wasted food esentially wastes valuable resources like water and farmland, one way to avoid this is to only buy what's enough for you, avoid buying too much food that would otherwise be consumed by someone who needs it.

3. Grow some plants

Simply buying plants and taking care of them will greatly benefit the climate since they are responsible in reusing carbon emmissions coming from us and in return giving us oxygen, in a larger scale, if everyone decided to take care of plants, we could easily fix the depreciating amount of plants around the world

4. Replace inefficient light bulbs

Light bulbs, at first doesn't look like something that would affect the climate, but due to the sheer amount of lightbulbs being used in the world, it becomes a problem if most are old and inefficient, make sure that the light bulbs your using are not old and replace them with newer, more efficient ones to help the climate.

5. Lessen electronic activity

As much as possible, it would be great for the climate if we manage how much electricity we use and wonder if we need to use them when not needed, example is an unnecessarily turned on light, do you really need that light to be on? Give your surroundings a check and turn off these kinds of situations.

6. Lessen the use of cars and motors

To lessen the amount of fossil fuel emmissions, try using cars or motorcycles less, if your destination is farely close to you, you could try to just walk or ride a bike, small actions like these can greatly lessen your carbon footprint and also give you some excercise.

7. Recycle

Recycling can greatly help reduce the amount of waste being thrown in out, and can promote a healthier way of living to those around you, it can also be a pretty fun activity when you find ways to repurpose something that would otherwise be thrown out.

8. Try to use renewable sources of energy

Getting your electricity from renewable sources like solar and wind can help promote for everyone to avoid using services that uses coal or oil, and will greately help lessen the impact of climate change due to our energy sources.

9. Conserve the use of Water

It may not seem like it, but conserving water does actually help out in reducing the impact of climate change, by not carelessly using water, it lowers the amount of energy needed to heat and pump water to our homes, energy that produces greenhouse gases, so make sure to only use water when needed and turn faucets off when it's not necessary

10. Eat more Fruits and Vegetables

Eating less meat and more fruits and vegetables helps out in reducing the impact of climate change by lessening the demand of livestock, farms for livestock create greenhouse gasses and also uses a lot of land and water. Another benefit of doing this is you'll be more healthy and save some money from buying meat.
